"Try to remain calm as much as possible"

Remaining calm is a very useful type of way to release any type of anger, anxieties, sadness and many more but what happens when that anxiety or anger or any afraidness takes over, and you can’t calm down? Here are my top 3 most helpful and useful tips you can try to use to help you calm down

Three ways to help you focus on yourself more

Deep breathing

For the frist tip we have here, is called "Deep Breathing". When you’re anxious or angry, you tend to take quick, shallow breaths. Dehorty says this sends a message to your brain, causing a positive feedback loop reinforcing your fight-or-flight response. That’s why taking long, deep calming breaths disrupts that loop and helps you calm down. Take slow, deep breaths. Inhale deeply through your nose, hold for a few seconds, and then exhale slowly through your mouth. Repeat several times until you start to feel more relaxed.

Take a break

For the second tip we have here, is called "Take a break from everything". If possible, step away from the stressful situation that you may face for a few hours. For example, if you are facing any troubles or difficulities through social media or anything. It may be hard at first so, we recommend you to try taking a short walk, grab some headphones and tune in to your favorite music, do any physical exercises or engage in a relaxing activity to give yourself a mental break.

Seek social support

For the last third tip we have here, is called "Seek social support". Releasing everything out of what you are facing right now to a trusted friend, family member, or anyone can provide an emotional support and help you feel safer and better rather than keeping everything into yourself. If you get into any types of cyberbullying online, or any types of troubles in realife, it is better and safer to talk or report to someone about it so that they can help you feel better.

If we take our time and start practicing these steps, they will eventually help you out. So, we would consider remaining calm as our first recommendations to be apart of our Preventions for the cyberbullying. Do not freak out, be sad or hesitate whenever cyberbullying happens, keep calm first.
