Types of cyberbullying

Here are some common cyberbullying that is happening on internet

Verbal Bullying

Verbal cyberbullying means being mean or threatening by using words.

This type of bullying can hurt a lot. It can make people feel upset, worried or very sad, and sometimes even think about hurting themselves. Not just the person being bullied, but also others who see it happen can be affected.

Learn more

Trolling

Online trolls are troublemakers on the internet. They purposefully post mean or annoying comments to get a reaction from others. They often hide behind fake names or profiles to avoid consequences.

Learn more

Doxxing

When someone digs up and shares private information about another person online without their permission. It could include things like their address, phone number, or even personal photos. It's a big invasion of privacy and can be used to harass or intimidate people.

Learn more

Spreading false rumours

It's when someone tells lies or untrue stories about someone else, often to harm their reputation or cause trouble. .

Learn more

Social Exclusion

This is when you are ignored or excluded when online. It would make you feel left out and alone.

Learn more

Impersonation

When the assaulter is pretending to be someone else to not get the blame on them but on the person they impersonate.

Learn more
